# Evidence-Based Sprint Session Generation

## Overview
Sprint sessions are now generated using evidence-based protocols from `sprint-training-knowledge.service.ts` and `FLAG_FOOTBALL_TRAINING_SCIENCE.md`.

## Phase-Based Sprint Protocol Selection

### Foundation Phase
- **Protocols**: Short acceleration, Deceleration training
- **Hill Sprints**: ✅ YES
- **Stair Sprints**: ❌ NO
- **Volume**: 15-25 sprints/week
- **Focus**: Movement quality over intensity

### Strength Accumulation Phase
- **Protocols**: Short acceleration, Resisted acceleration, Deceleration training
- **Hill Sprints**: ✅ YES
- **Stair Sprints**: ❌ NO
- **Volume**: 20-30 sprints/week
- **Focus**: Horizontal force development

### Power Development Phase
- **Protocols**: Short acceleration, Resisted acceleration, Flying sprints
- **Hill Sprints**: ❌ NO
- **Stair Sprints**: ❌ NO
- **Volume**: 25-35 sprints/week
- **Focus**: Maximum velocity development

### Speed Development Phase
- **Protocols**: Short acceleration, Flying sprints, In-and-out sprints, Repeated sprint ability
- **Hill Sprints**: ❌ NO
- **Stair Sprints**: ❌ NO
- **Volume**: 30-45 sprints/week (peak volume)
- **Focus**: Speed endurance and RSA

### Competition Phase
- **Protocols**: Short acceleration, Deceleration training
- **Hill Sprints**: ❌ NO
- **Stair Sprints**: ❌ NO
- **Volume**: 15-25 sprints/week
- **Focus**: Maintenance, games provide stimulus

### Mid-Season Reload Phase (July)
- **Protocols**: Short acceleration, Resisted acceleration, Flying sprints, Speed endurance, **Stair sprints** (if ACWR >= 0.8)
- **Hill Sprints**: ✅ YES
- **Stair Sprints**: ✅ YES (if ACWR >= 0.8 and athlete well-conditioned)
- **Volume**: 30-45 sprints/week
- **Focus**: Build "extra layer" for second half of season

### Peak Phase
- **Protocols**: Short acceleration, Flying sprints
- **Hill Sprints**: ❌ NO
- **Stair Sprints**: ❌ NO
- **Volume**: 20-30 sprints/week
- **Focus**: Sharpness and explosiveness

## Stair Sprint Requirements

Stair sprints are **ADVANCED** and only included when:
1. **Phase**: Mid-Season Reload (July)
2. **ACWR**: >= 0.8 (athlete well-conditioned)
3. **Training Base**: 12+ months sprint training
4. **Prerequisites**:
   - Single-leg squat with control
   - 30+ consecutive bodyweight squats
   - Nordic curl competency
   - Consistent sprint training for 8+ weeks
   - No lower body injuries in past 6 weeks

**Variations**:
- Single-step: Every step, maximum speed (intermediate)
- Double-step: Skip every other step (advanced)
- Lateral bounds: Side-to-side up stairs (advanced)

## Hill Sprint Guidelines

Hill sprints are used in:
- Foundation phase
- Strength Accumulation phase
- Mid-Season Reload phase

**Purpose**: Develop horizontal force and acceleration mechanics
**Grade**: 6-12% incline optimal
**Technique**: Drive knees high, stay on balls of feet, pump arms aggressively

## Sprint Warmup Protocol

For sprint sessions, warmup includes (in order):
1. **A-March**: Knee drive mechanics (2 sets)
2. **A-Skip**: Rhythm and coordination (2 sets × 10 reps)
3. **B-Skip**: Pawing action (2 sets × 10 reps)
4. **High Knees**: Knee drive frequency (2 sets × 30s)
5. **Butt Kicks**: Hamstring recovery (2 sets × 30s)
6. **Toy Soldiers**: Hamstring flexibility dynamic (2 sets)
7. **Hamstring Stretch**: 30s each leg
8. **Pogo Jumps**: Ankle stiffness (3 sets × 20 reps) - Essential for sprint performance

**QB/Center Note**: Wall slides are skipped on sprint days - sprint warmup takes priority.

## Exercise Selection Logic

1. **Check Phase**: Map periodization phase to sprint phase guidelines
2. **Check ACWR**: For stair sprints, require ACWR >= 0.8
3. **Select Protocols**: Based on phase guidelines
4. **Query Exercises**: Search for exercises matching protocol types
5. **Prioritize**: Acceleration > Hill/Stair > Flying > Deceleration
6. **Set Parameters**: Sets, reps, rest based on protocol type

## Evidence Base

- **Haugen et al. (2019)**: Sprint interval training for elite team-sport athletes
- **Morin & Samozino (2016)**: Horizontal force determines acceleration
- **Clark et al. (2019)**: 0-10 yards = 60% of 40-yard variance
- **Kubo et al. (2000)**: Achilles tendon stiffness correlates with sprint performance
- **Petrakos et al. (2016)**: 10-20% BW sled load optimal for resisted sprints

## Safety Considerations

- **Stair Sprints**: Only for well-conditioned athletes (ACWR >= 0.8, 12+ months training)
- **Hill Sprints**: Use in Foundation/Strength/Mid-Season phases only
- **Flying Sprints**: Avoid if hamstring tightness or excessive CNS fatigue
- **RSA Training**: Too fatiguing for competition weeks
- **Speed Endurance**: Off-season only, avoid within 7 days of competition
